Privacy policy

Effective July 16, 2026

The short version: this site collects almost nothing, and we'd like to keep it that way. Here's the whole picture, in plain language.

Who we are

Calidescope LLC, a California limited liability company. For anything in this policy: bret@calidescope.llc.

What we collect

If you just visit: this is a static website. It has no accounts, no forms, and sets no tracking cookies. Our hosting provider keeps standard server logs (IP address, browser type, pages requested, timestamps) for security and reliability, as essentially every website's host does. We don't use those logs to identify or profile you.

Fonts: served from this site directly — visiting doesn't send your IP address to Google or any other font network.

If you email us: we get your email address and whatever you write. We use it to reply and to work together if that's where it goes. We don't sell it, share it, or add you to any list you didn't ask for.

If you subscribe to something (a newsletter, when one exists): we'll collect your email with your consent, use it only for that subscription, and every issue will carry a working unsubscribe. Consent first, always.

Cookies & analytics

Today: none. No analytics, no advertising pixels, no tracking cookies.

If we ever add measurement (for example, analytics or a LinkedIn Insight Tag to see whether our posts bring anyone here), it will be opt-in: a consent banner will ask first, nothing will load unless you agree, declining is a one-click equal to accepting, and your choice will be remembered for 12 months. You can change your mind any time via the "Cookie preferences" link in the footer. The only thing stored before consent is the record of your choice itself, kept in your own browser.

What we never do

We don't sell personal information. We don't share it for cross-context behavioral advertising. We don't buy lists, scrape inboxes, or add anyone to outreach they didn't invite.

Your rights

Depending on where you live (the EU/UK under GDPR, California under CCPA/CPRA, and a growing list of other states), you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or export personal information we hold, and to object to certain processing. Our policy is simpler than the map: email us and we'll honor any reasonable request regardless of your jurisdiction, within 30 days. Since we hold almost nothing, most requests are short conversations.

Third parties, retention, children

Links on this site (for example to throughline.builders or LinkedIn) lead to services with their own privacy policies. Email correspondence is retained as long as it's useful to our working relationship, then deleted in the ordinary course. This site is a business site, not directed at children under 16, and we don't knowingly collect their information.

Changes

If this policy changes materially — especially if we ever turn on measurement — we'll update the effective date above and the consent banner will do its job before anything else does.
