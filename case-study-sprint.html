PROOF · SPRINT CORPORATION
Forecasting demand before the tools existed to do it.
1995–1999
ALSO READS AS PERFORM
SITUATION
Sprint needed to forecast call-center demand for one of the largest telecoms in America.
That kind of statistical rigor barely existed in marketing organizations yet, and neither did the infrastructure to support it at scale.
TASK
Build the forecasting models — and eventually the data infrastructure — to support marketing decisions at telecom scale.
ACTION
Built econometric time-series models forecasting demand across Sprint Long Distance and Sprint Local, then built the database-marketing datamart serving both businesses — 27GB, which in the late ’90s was enough to need its own data room — progressing through four roles in under four years as the mandate grew.
